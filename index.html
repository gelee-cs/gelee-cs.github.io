Biography

Autonomous Data Systems · Semantic Processing · Cost-Aware

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at RMIT University, supervised by Prof. Zhifeng Bao and Dr. Shixun Huang. Alongside RMIT, I work with Dr. Yanchang Zhao at CSIRO Data61 and I am currently a visiting researcher in the Data Science discipline at The University of Queensland.

AI is moving fast, but real deployments still start with the same bottleneck — data. The hard part is rarely the model. It is finding the right sources, understanding what they really contain, fixing quality issues, and stitching pieces together into something a model, or a decision, can rely on.

My research sits at the intersection of modern database systems and AI and machine learning. I develop agentic workflows for data preparation and management over uncurated data. This includes profiling raw assets, semantically discovering what is relevant, and assembling and integrating across heterogeneous sources. I am working toward more autonomous database capabilities where data management is dependable under real budgets. That means fast enough to use (time and latency), affordable enough to run (monetary cost and computing resources), and reliable enough to trust (traceable evidence and error bounds).
